The Compost Monitoring System

Get Started

What is composting?

Composting is a natural process that involves the decomposition of organic materials, such as food scraps, yard waste, and leaves, to produce nutrient-rich compost. It is a sustainable way to recycle organic waste into a valuable soil amendment.

Who can compost?

Anyone can compost! Individuals, households, businesses, schools and community gardens. Composting is a flexible and scalable practice that can be adapted to various settings and needs.

Where can we compost?

Composting can be done in different locations. Including backyard composting at home, composting in community gardens or schools, workplace composting programs, and utilizing commercial composting facilities.

Why is composting so important?

It reduces the amount of organic waste sent to landfills, reducing methane emissions and mitigating environmental harm. Also enriches soil health, improves water retention, reduces the need for synthetic fertilizers, and promotes sustainable gardening and agriculture.

Why does composting need surveillance?

To ensure the composting process is successful and efficient. Surveillance helps maintain the ideal conditions for decomposition and helps also identify and address potential issues like odor, pests, or imbalances in the compost pile. By implementing surveillance, composters can optimize the composting process, prevent problems, and produce high-quality compost.

OUR surveillance solution !

Our surveillance solution uses LoRa technology to capture data from nodes strategically placed within the composting site. This data is wirelessly transmitted to a gateway, which serves as a bridge between the nodes and the cloud. From there, the data is securely sent to the cloud for further analysis and processing.

Through our intuitive web application, composting managers can access real-time data visualizations, including interactive charts and a comprehensive dashboard. This enables them to closely monitor crucial composting metrics and make informed decisions to optimize the composting process effectively.
